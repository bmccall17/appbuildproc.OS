Process states

The model is a loop, not a waterfall.

Projects can occupy multiple states at once, skip states that do not match their pressure, or revisit a state when new evidence appears.

Timeline and corpus

A shared process across different project types.

The same operating pattern appears in public apps, local tools, games, publication systems, and meta-process products. The timing changes with project pressure.

Pattern strength

The strongest findings are pressure-driven.

Frequency is not treated as a universal checklist. It shows which process moves repeat and where caveats matter.

Productive chaos vs. avoidable rework

Productive

  • Fast exposure to reality.
  • Creative pivots with reversibility.
  • Instrumentation before full content.
  • Public reflection as build fuel.

Avoidable

  • Duplicate sources of truth.
  • Late guardrails after known risk.
  • Stale human-facing docs.
  • Dirty working trees as hidden history.
